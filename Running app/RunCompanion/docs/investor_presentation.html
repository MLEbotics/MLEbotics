2. What Was Discussed (Product Scope)

Area What the Product Does
Pace Robot Leads runner on GPS waypoints at target speed, with adaptive slowdown and safe stop if runner falls behind.
Voice Control Owner-only voice commands for stop, speed up/down, pause/resume, return to start, alerting, and music control.
Voice Coaching Spoken pace feedback, milestone encouragement, obstacle notices, battery warnings, and workout status summaries.
People Alerting One tap or voice trigger sends SMS to saved route-ahead contacts and plays speaker announcement to clear path.
Garmin Integration Reads BLE heart rate, cadence, speed, and distance from Garmin watch services.
Run Data Saves workout sessions to Firestore with duration, distance, pace, HR and cadence metrics.
AI Companion Gemini-powered conversational coach that can embed control commands for robot actions.

3. Voice + Alerting Details (Important Investor Differentiator)

Voice Commands Supported

Stop, speed up, slow down, pause, resume, distance, pace, status, open box, close box, play music, alert ahead, clear left, clear right, return to start.

People-Ahead Alert Flow

  1. Runner taps alert button or says a trigger phrase.
  2. Robot speaker announces runner is approaching.
  3. App opens SMS to each saved contact with distance/ETA/message.

Directional Messaging

  • Clear Left: asks people to move right
  • Clear Right: asks people to move left
  • Default: generic polite clear-route request

4. Robot Behavior + Speed Logic

Condition Robot Response
Runner 0–5 m behind Maintain full target pace
Runner 5–8 m behind Reduce to 60% of target pace
Runner >8 m behind for 3+ seconds Stop; show runner-lost status in app
Obstacle <50 cm ahead Stop, servo sweep, choose clearer side
Both sides blocked Reverse slightly and rescan
Waypoint complete Stop and mark workout complete
Speed framework discussed: target pace is runner-selected; firmware and app support up to 30 km/h; speed can be adjusted by voice and by app controls during a run.

6. App Architecture Decision (Including Your Hybrid Point)

Approach Pros Cons Fit for This Product
Fully Native / Standard Flutter app Strong device integration, best runtime control Store release needed for most updates Excellent for robot control, BLE, and low-latency workflows
WebView-heavy app Fast cloud updates Weaker hardware integration and performance Not ideal for critical real-time control loops
Hybrid frameworks loading cloud modules (Flutter/React Native/Ionic) Better performance than WebView; supports cloud-driven updates; usually app-store friendlier than full dynamic code push Higher architecture complexity; requires secure module-loading/versioning system Best middle-ground for this product as it scales enterprise features

Recommended Plan

Keep robot control, BLE, safety logic, and voice pipeline in stable app binaries. Move non-critical UI modules and content surfaces to cloud-loaded modules over time.
This preserves real-time safety/performance while enabling faster product iteration.
